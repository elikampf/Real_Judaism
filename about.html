My Journey

1

Modern Orthodox Roots in Queens

My path began with a Modern Orthodox upbringing in Queens, leading to nearly two decades of dedication to practical Jewish learning.

2

Semicha from Ohr Hachaim

After receiving semicha from Yeshivas Ohr Hachaim and studying under Rabbi Mordechai Finkelman, I developed my signature approach: making profound Torah accessible without sacrificing depth.

3

Maggid Shiur at Ateres Yaakov

As a Maggid Shiur at Yeshivas Ateres Yaakov, I transform complex Iyun Shiur into dynamic explorations that connect directly to daily life. My students know me for revealing the immediate relevance of complex lomdus‚Äîwhat I call "practical hashkafa."

4

Certified Mohel & Chosson Teacher

Beyond the beis medrash, I serve as a certified mohel and chosson teacher. My pre-marriage guidance has become particularly sought after.

5

Started Recording Shiurim

The shift to recording came reluctantly‚Äîworried recordings would limit frank discussion of sensitive topics. But recognizing these conversations were needed by many, I began sharing more broadly.

6

Settled in Ramat Beit Shemesh

What started as internal recordings became a library covering everything from Shmiras Einayim to modern technological challenges. Living in Ramat Beit Shemesh with my family since 2007.

My Approach

My teaching philosophy centers on a simple but profound truth that guides everything I do:

"The most important thing is not to teach people what they don't know, but to remind them what they know very well‚Äîand help them live it."

What this means in practice: Most people already know the fundamentals of Torah living‚Äîthey've learned the basics, understand the concepts, and recognize what they should do. The real challenge isn't acquiring more knowledge, but applying what we already know to our daily lives.

This is why my shiurim focus on making Torah concepts immediately relevant to modern challenges. Whether it's navigating technology and purity, building healthy marriages, or finding meaning in difficult times, I help people bridge the gap between knowing and doing.

Rather than presenting Torah as abstract theory, I show how ancient wisdom provides practical solutions for real-world problems. This approach has helped hundreds of students transform their understanding into action.
